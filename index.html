About me —
between mist & stillness

I’m Aiom, a 16-year-old from the heart of Meghalaya — a place where clouds rest on pine forests and rains whisper stories. I find beauty in unspoken emotions, analog photography, handwritten letters, and the slow rhythm of nature. My world is built on contrasts: light and shadow, noise and quiet, tradition and self-discovery.

I spend my days journaling, reading philosophy under grey skies, and documenting fragments of life through a monochrome lens. This portfolio is not a resume — it’s a mirror. I’m not defined by jobs or metrics; I’m defined by curiosity, heritage, and the courage to stay authentic. Meghalaya taught me patience, and the hills taught me that silence is a language too.

I believe in creating for the sake of meaning, not attention. My influences range from old cinema to minimal poetry. I draw, I write, I observe — and I’m learning every day how to turn solitude into art. This is me, unfiltered.

poetry & prose

The roots I carry

Meghalaya isn’t just a location — it’s my anchor. Growing up amidst the wettest lands on earth, I learned resilience. The living root bridges, the jazz of raindrops on tin roofs, and the Khasi matrilineal culture shaped my worldview. I’m proud to represent a land that breathes ancient traditions, and I carry its stories through every quiet step I take.

What inspires me

Black-and-white cinema, minimalist architecture, the works of Saul Leiter, and the raw energy of indie folk. I find art in ordinary corners: fogged windows, worn-out books, old family portraits. I don't chase trends — I chase authenticity. And that’s the only aesthetic I live by.
